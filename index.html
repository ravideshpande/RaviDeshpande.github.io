Jatakas

These are the tales of the Jatakas. These are moral stories written in the 4th century, probably 50 to 150 years after the death of Gautama Buddha. Some of the stories are said to be variations of the stories told by Aesop in the 7th century B.C.

The Deer, Turtle, and Woodpecker

This story shows that 'a friend in need is a friend indeed'. A Deer, Woodpecker, and Turtle take turns saving each other from an evil hunter.

Deer goes to School

This story shows the importance of paying attention in class. A Deer uses what he learns to escape the grasps of death.

Akbar & Birbal

These are the stories of Akbar and Birbal. Akbar's grandfather, Babur arrived in India and fought the famous battle of Panipat against the Lodi dynasty, and won. He then fought Rana Sanga, and took the drastic measure of swearing not to drink wine to defeat him and secure his position in India. Akbar was born under a lucky star - three enemies were vanquished within 18 months. When Akbar was king, he met a wise man named Birbal and asked him to adorn his court as one of the famous nine gems. The following stories show Birbal's wit when tested by the Emperor.

Akbar & Birbal - The Seal

This story shows the importance of knowing one's responsibilities and Birbal's wit in arousing the Emperor of this fact.

Akbar & Birbal - Trip to Burma

This story once again displays Birbal's wit - this time to save his and the Emperor's nephew's life.

Gopal the Jester

These are the stories of Gopal the Jester. Gopal was a jester in medieval Bengal, under King Krishnachandra, the king of Nadia. Gopal amused the court with his humour and wit.

Gopal Measures the Earth

This story shows how Gopal uses his wit to save his king from the lazy Nawab of Murshidabad.
